About Me

I'm a neuroscientist based in San Francisco. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and neurological disease — I'm motivated by the question of how we can use data and algorithms to understand and predict disease progression at a scale that clinical observation alone cannot reach.

Currently at the Baraban Lab (UCSF Department of Neurosurgery), I investigate the role of microglia in regulating MGE progenitor development in mouse epilepsy models. I previously worked in the Seeley Lab (UCSF Memory and Aging Center), generating neuron tracings to train ML models for automated segmentation of FTD/ALS postmortem tissue, and in the Larkum Lab at Humboldt University Berlin, conducting advanced image analysis on cortical layer 5 pyramidal neurons.

I hold a B.S. in Natural Sciences from Minerva University and am a first-author in the Journal of Alzheimer's Disease. I serve as an Associate Editor and Peer Reviewer at the same journal.

Alzheimer's Disease MCI Conversion Predictor

A dual-model machine learning system predicting whether a patient with Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) will convert to dementia within 24 months — using only baseline clinical data. The central finding: accessible cognitive tests predict conversion as reliably as expensive CSF biomarkers, enabling equitable prediction in resource-limited settings.

  • Auto-routing dual-model tool: selects 26-feature or 16-feature model based on available tests
